Game Instructions!

The Game Lore

After all wars the world collaped into a massive desert. Inside this beast the only thing that could exist is pressure.

Players Movement

  • INS

Player One:

The right and left arrows move the player along the level. Use up near a wall to climb it. Not to fly all over the level! Ins is used to jump a moderate humanly possible amount.

  • W
  • A
  • S
  • D

Player Two:

The A and D arrows move the player along the level. Use W near a wall to climb it. Not to fly all over the level! SPACE is used to jump a moderate humanly possible amount.

Use the arrows left and right to control your character run to the left or right.

Use the up arrow near a wall to climb it.

The down arrow does not do anything... for now!

Press spacebar to jump a moderate humanly possible amount.

Rules! So many, ermmm... few!

About the Game Idea

  • This game was offered as a starting project for the Full Stack Web Development bootcamp, by Ironhack. The idea consisted of practicing our fresh Frontend and Canvas skills with a free theme. I've pushed the boundaries a little bit further, once I was feeling comfortable with JavaScript and Canvas.
  • Thus the idea could be improved to a level where we could experience fome kind of innovation, diverting someway of the status quo:
  • We've always wished to feel music, travel along it within our memories and feelings. This game is a tribute to this desire, a sinestesic feel to music and sound. We try to imagine sound, we figured out how its fisics works but we've never climbed it, jumped over it, exhilirate ourselves by touching it.

Methodology

  • Conceptual Design, at this phase the main idea of the game was iterated among several possibilities, but tagging the possibilites of p5.sound. These concepts involved platform, rpg, GuitarHero clones with storytelling (a Star Wars storytelling) and sound making with envelopes. At the end, observing the time and scope restrictions, it was decided to make this platform game. Given that this generates level topology based on the music amplitude distribution over time, thus building unique levels.
  • Preliminar Degisn was conducted with testing in mind. The development here was driven, with some extention of planning, by trial and error. This approach revealed time consumming but very important for building algorithm coding skills for gaming and DOM manipulation. It also improved creativity to implement new game mechanics.
  • And, finally, Detailed Design and Prototype Deployment. At this level the MVP was prepared to be deployed on GitHub pages. Code was organized and distributed among meaningull files and classes and, for the new platform, new debugging tooks place.
